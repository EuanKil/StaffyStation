“Luna the Adventure Buddy” – Kieran, Auckland “Luna’s happiest place is beside me, whether we’re trekking in the Waitakeres or paddling at Piha Beach. Staffys get a reputation for being energetic, but Luna channels all her energy into play—she’s obsessed with fetching driftwood. She’s also known for doing Staffy ‘zoomies’ around the backyard, leaving us in stitches. After any excitement, Luna’s quickest to claim a spot on the couch for cuddles. The best part? She senses when I’m stressed and always finds a way to make me laugh.”

“From Rescue to Family” – Tania & Mo, Christchurch “We adopted Rocco from a rescue and, to be honest, didn’t know what to expect. Turns out, Staffys are incredibly resilient and loving. Rocco’s story began with challenges—he was timid at first, flinching at loud noises. With time, he’s flourished into a confident, snuggly companion who adores our toddler. Their bond is special: she shares her biscuits, he shares his toys. Rocco changed our home, showing us patience, trust, and pure Staffy affection.”

“Buddy the Gentle Guardian” – Aria, Tauranga “Buddy’s got the classic Staffy grin and the softest heart. He keeps an eye on our kids in the backyard and gently nudges them away from puddles. Our favourite weekend is sunrise at Mount Maunganui—Buddy sits with us at the top like he owns the view. A lickimat with peanut butter has been a lifesaver for rainy days and calm-time training.”

“Poppy’s First Tramping Trip” – Josh & Hana, Dunedin “We took Poppy on her first overnight tramp along the Silver Peaks. She carried a tiny pack with her collapsible bowl and treats—looked very official! We kept the pace easy and stopped often for water. Staffys are sturdy but can run hot, so we hike early and pack a cooling bandana. She slept the whole drive home, snoring the cutest Staffy snores.”
